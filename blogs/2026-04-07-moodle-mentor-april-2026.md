    <div class=" flexi-block">        <h2 id="block-h2-your-free-pass-into-the-moodleverse">Your free pass into the Moodleverse</h2>
  <div class="quote">
    <div class="text">How can I benefit from your services? I want to gain skills. Can I use your free programs?</div>
          <div class="name">Abdullah A.</div>
      </div>
  <div class="text"><p><b>Moodle Mentor says:</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Great question, Abdullah! So the best place to start is </span><a href="https://moodle.academy/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>Moodle Academy</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> — a free library of courses covering everything from online teaching strategies and course design basics to more advanced Moodle skills for administrators and developers.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">From there, </span><a href="https://moodle.org/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>moodle.org</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is your home for the </span><a href="https://moodle.org/course/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">community forums</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, where Moodlers around the world share knowledge, answer questions, and solve problems together.  You can also find </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/Main_page" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Moodle Docs</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, which is the go-to reference for how everything works.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you want something more formal to show for your learning, it&#8217;s worth knowing about the </span><a href="https://moodle.com/meq/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>Moodle Educator Qualification</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and the </span><a href="https://moodle.com/maq/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>Moodle Administrator Qualification</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> — structured pathways that lead to a recognised credential. Both are available through Moodle and our Certified Partners.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For organisations that want more customised support — implementation, training, consultancy, or hosting — that&#8217;s where </span><a href="https://moodle.com/services/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Moodle Services</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> come in. While there&#8217;s plenty you can learn and do for free, working with a team of experts can end up saving a lot of time, money, and effort in the long run.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">My advice is to start with Moodle Academy and go from there. We’re happy you’re here!</span></p>

    <div class=" flexi-block">        <h2 id="block-h2-can-i-borrow-your-resource">Can I borrow your resource?</h2>
  <div class="quote">
    <div class="text">Is the excellent material in the guide to Bridging the hidden learning gap available under a (free) license that allows me to use parts of it in trainings for teachers at our university?
</div>
          <div class="name">Malte S.</div>
      </div>
  <div class="text"><p><b>Moodle Mentor says:</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Malte, it&#8217;s wonderful to hear the guide has been useful — and that you want to share it with your faculty.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Go for it! You can find the guide here: </span><a href="https://services.moodle.com/hidden-learning-gap" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Bridging the hidden learning gap: Designing a digital learning environment that works for everyone</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. We&#8217;re always happy to see it making its way into educator training — that&#8217;s exactly the spirit it was written in.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The guide grew out of years of experience supporting diverse, large-scale learning programmes. Its core idea is simple but important: wider access to online learning doesn&#8217;t automatically mean everyone has the same experience. Learners bring different levels of digital confidence and support required, as well as different home environments — and those hidden distinctions directly shape how they engage and whether they succeed. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The guide helps course designers see where those gaps might be, and make smarter, more human-centred design choices to close them. It works regardless of which platform or sector you&#8217;re in, which is part of why we love seeing it travel.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you do use it with your faculty, we&#8217;d love an attribution — but mostly we just hope it&#8217;s helpful.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">P.S. We recently ran a webinar diving into practical strategies from the guide — you can watch the on-demand recording here: </span><a href="https://services.moodle.com/hidden-learning-gap-webinar" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Meeting learners where they are: Practical ways to close the hidden learning gap</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
</div>

    <div class=" flexi-block">        <h2 id="block-h2-lost-in-translation-moodles-got-you">Lost in translation? Moodle&#8217;s got you</h2>
  <div class="quote">
    <div class="text">How does the Moodle platform support multiple languages? How can the instructor help people with diverse languages during office hours or via video/audio?</div>
          <div class="name">R. E.</div>
      </div>
  <div class="text"><p><b>Moodle Mentor says:</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Moodle platforms are used in over 234 countries and available in over 200 languages. With strong multilingual capabilities built in, this is well-trodden ground.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">At the platform level, each user can set their preferred language and the entire Moodle site interface — menus, buttons, etc. — will display in that language. These interface translations are handled through </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/Language_packs" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">language packs</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, which are free to install. If your language is supported (and chances are it is), your site can feel native to learners regardless of where they are.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Beyond the platform interface, the </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/Multi-language_content_filter" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Multi-language content filter</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> allows you to add content in multiple languages within a single course, displaying the right version to each learner based on their language preference. So a single course page can show English to one learner and Spanish to another — no duplicate courses required.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For live sessions — office hours, video calls, tutorials — Moodle LMS integrates with tools like </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/BigBlueButton" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">BigBlueButton</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, which has live captioning and auto-translation capabilities. Many educators also use external tools like Google Meet or Zoom, which have built-in live translation and captioning features, and link to them from within their Moodle course. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The honest answer on supporting diverse languages during live sessions is that the technology can help, but it works best when paired with thoughtful facilitation — clear speech, visual aids, and patience go a long way alongside any tool.</span></p>
</div>
</div>
  </div>

    <div class=" flexi-block">        <h2 id="block-h2-getting-h5p-to-play-nicely-with-your-grades">Getting H5P to play nicely with your grades</h2>
  <div class="quote">
    <div class="text">How do you integrate H5P results to the gradebook? Or would you recommend using the native assessment tools in Moodle LMS?
</div>
          <div class="name">Anathi N.</div>
      </div>
  <div class="text"><p><b>Moodle Mentor says:</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Good news: you don&#8217;t necessarily have to choose.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">H5P activities created using the </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/H5P_activity" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>H5P activity module</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> do pass results to the </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/Grader_report" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Moodle gradebook</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> — completion and scores are recorded and appear alongside your other graded activities. So if you&#8217;re using H5P and want grades to count, make sure you&#8217;re using the activity module version and that grades are enabled in the activity settings.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">That said, Moodle&#8217;s native assessment tools — for example, </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/Quiz_activity" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Quiz</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/Assignment_activity" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Assignment</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/Workshop_activity" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Workshop</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> — are generally more powerful when it comes to gradebook integration, detailed reporting, and features like question banks, rubrics, and submission workflows. If grades and analytics matter, native tools give you more to work with.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Most of the clients I’ve worked with over the years use H5P when they want engaging, interactive content — branching scenarios, drag-and-drop, interactive video. And then native Moodle activities where assessment rigour, feedback, and gradebook flexibility are the priority. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">But remember, with Moodle solutions, you can have it all — these activities all work well together in the same course, so don&#8217;t be afraid to mix and match depending on your desired outcomes.</span></p>
</div>
</div>

    <div class=" flexi-block">        <h2 id="block-h2-so-you-want-to-teach-languages-online">So you want to teach languages online</h2>
  <div class="quote">
    <div class="text">What tools do people use to facilitate language learning in Moodle platforms? We are a small language school just starting to create courses online, and are looking for tips to get started.</div>
          <div class="name">Lisa C.</div>
      </div>
  <div class="text"><p><b>Moodle Mentor says:</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Welcome to online language teaching, Lisa — and welcome to Moodle! You&#8217;ve picked a platform with a lot to offer for language learning specifically.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A few places to start:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>For general language learning</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">, Moodle Certified Integration </span><a href="https://moodle.com/certified-integrations/poodll/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>PoodLL</b></a> <span style="font-weight: 400;">makes tools widely used in language education. PoodLL’s tools add audio and video recording directly into activities, so learners can record themselves speaking and submit it as an assignment. PoodLL is also great for vocabulary learning and assessment of speaking and reading — which makes it my all-around language learning recommendation.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>For interactive practice</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/H5P_activity" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">H5P</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is genuinely excellent for language courses — fill-in-the-blank, drag-and-drop vocabulary, dialogue cards, and branching conversation scenarios are all possible without any coding. It&#8217;s free and built into Moodle LMS.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>For live conversation practice</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/BigBlueButton" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">BigBlueButton</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> or any LTI-connected video conferencing tool lets you run small group conversation sessions directly from within your course.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">My biggest tip for a small school just starting out: don&#8217;t try to build everything at once. Start with one course, keep the structure simple, and experiment with different tools as you go. You&#8217;ll learn a lot from your first group of learners about what to build next.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Good luck — and write back to let us know how it goes!</span></p>
