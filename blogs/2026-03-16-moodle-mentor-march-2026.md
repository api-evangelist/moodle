    <div class=" flexi-block">        <h2 id="block-h2-what-makes-a-moodle-course-beautiful">What makes a Moodle course beautiful?</h2>
  <div class="quote">
    <div class="text">How can I create beautiful courses?
</div>
          <div class="name">Londeka N.</div>
      </div>
  <div class="text"><p><b>Moodle Mentor says:</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Ah, the eternal question.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The tricky part is that </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">beautiful</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"> can mean very different things to different people. For some, it’s visual design. For others, it’s clarity, flow, or how easy a course feels to navigate. I’d love to hear more about what a beautiful course means to you.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">That said, one thing I’ve learned after years of designing courses in Moodle platforms is that beauty usually starts with </span><b>structure</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">When a course feels clear, welcoming, and easy to navigate, learners experience it as “beautiful” — even before any colours or images come into play.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A few simple practices can make a big difference:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Start with a clear structure </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">— Organise your <a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/Course_homepage" rel="noopener" target="_blank">course homepage</a> into meaningful </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">sections</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (and </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/Course_homepage#Add_a_subsection" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">subsections</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">!) with short, descriptive headings so learners can easily understand what they’re about to do.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Add visual anchors</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> —</span> <a href="https://docs.moodle.org/4x/sv/Course_settings#Course_image" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Course images</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, strategically placed </span><a href="https://moodle.org/plugins/filter_fontawesome" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">icons</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, or simple </span><a href="https://services.moodle.com/course-banner-tip" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">banners</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> can help learners orient themselves and quickly recognise where they are.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Reduce scrolling and clutter </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">— Long walls of content can feel overwhelming. Break materials into smaller pieces using </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/Page_resource" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Pages</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/Book_resource" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Books</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/Lesson_activity" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Lessons</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, or other short activities.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Guide the learner’s journey </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">— Use </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/Text_and_media_area" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Text and media areas</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, section descriptions, or activity instructions to explain what learners should do first, next, and why it matters.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you’re looking for a quick way to change the overall look and feel of your course, </span><a href="https://moodle.org/plugins/browse.php?list=category&amp;id=19" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>course format plugins</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> can also make a big difference. Formats like </span><a href="https://moodle.org/plugins/format_tiles" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Tiles</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> or </span><a href="https://moodle.org/plugins/format_onetopic" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">OneTopic</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> reorganise the course layout and navigation, making it quick and easy to give your course a little glow up.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The truth is, some of the most beautiful Moodle courses I’ve seen are actually quite simple — they just make the learning path obvious and welcoming.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Design for clarity first. The aesthetics will follow.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">And if you’re curious about how plugins can transform Moodle sites more broadly, here’s a blog post I wrote that includes some specific examples: </span><a href="https://moodle.com/us/news/make-moodle-lms-your-own-the-power-of-plugins/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Make Moodle LMS your own: The power of plugins</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Write back and show us your beautiful Moodle courses!</span></p>

    <div class=" flexi-block">        <h2 id="block-h2-paying-for-moodle-lms-but-missing-the-updates">Paying for Moodle LMS… but missing the updates</h2>
  <div class="quote">
    <div class="text">Good afternoon, I&#8217;m writing to mention that I&#8217;ve been a user of [Company] for a long time, but all these improvements and fixes aren&#8217;t reflected in [Company]. I have a subscription; it&#8217;s not free, but the improvements aren&#8217;t reaching [Company]. That&#8217;s all. Thank you.
</div>
          <div class="name">Igor C.</div>
      </div>
  <div class="text"><p><b>Moodle Mentor says:</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">First, a quick note: I’ve redacted the company name you mentioned in your question. <strong>[Company] is not a <a href="https://moodle.com/services/certified-service-providers/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Moodle Certified Partner</a>.</strong></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">What you’re describing is something we occasionally hear from organisations using Moodle LMS through </span><b>third-party providers that aren’t part of the </b><a href="https://moodle.com/services/certified-service-providers/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>Moodle Certified Partner network</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Moodle itself releases regular updates that include improvements, bug fixes, performance upgrades, and new features. However, whether those updates actually reach your platform <strong>depends on the </strong></span><b>service provider managing your site</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Some providers upgrade regularly and keep their clients current. Others may delay upgrades, limit access to newer features, or run heavily customised versions of Moodle LMS that are harder to maintain over time. When that happens, organisations can end up paying for a service that slowly drifts away from the current Moodle platform.</span></p>
<p><b>Working with </b><a href="https://moodle.com/services/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>Moodle directly</b> <b>for services</b></a> <span style="font-weight: 400;">helps avoid this problem. Certified Partners follow Moodle standards, stay aligned with official releases, and contribute back to the Moodle project. This helps ensure you receive ongoing improvements, security updates, and reliable support.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For smaller sites or organisations just getting started, </span><a href="https://www.moodlecloud.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>MoodleCloud</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is another way to run a Moodle site directly with hosting and updates managed by Moodle.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you’re concerned that your current provider isn’t keeping your platform up to date, it may be worth reviewing your options. </span><b>Our team would </b><a href="https://moodle.com/get-moodle/?utm_source=moodle_mentor&amp;utm_medium=blog&amp;utm_campaign=edit_mode" rel="noopener"><b>love to help you explore</b></a><b> what a migration or upgrade path could look like.</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">And Igor — thanks for raising the question. It’s an important one that more organisations run into than you might think.</span></p>
</div>
</div>

    <div class=" flexi-block">        <h2 id="block-h2-when-ai-enters-the-chat">When AI enters the chat</h2>
  <div class="quote">
    <div class="text">I want to implement an AI chatbot on my university&#8217;s platform. I&#8217;m an administrator. Is there any possibility of receiving tutoring or assistance to do this? Thank you.
</div>
          <div class="name">Alejandra V. I.</div>
      </div>
  <div class="text"><p><b>Moodle Mentor says:</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Implementing an AI chatbot on your site is definitely possible, but I understand why you’d seek out assistance in doing so — and we have experts ready to help.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Many Moodle sites are experimenting with AI chatbots to help learners navigate courses, answer common questions, or provide additional learning support. As an administrator, one common way to implement this is through </span><b>chatbot plugins.</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For example, plugins like </span><a href="https://moodle.org/plugins/block_openai_chat" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>AI Chat Block</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="https://moodle.org/plugins/block_ai_chat" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>AI Chat</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, or tools like </span><a href="https://moodle.org/plugins/local_asyntai" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>Asyntai AI Chatbot</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> can add a chat interface directly to your Moodle site where users can ask questions and receive AI-generated responses.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Many allow administrators to customise the chatbot’s personality, instructions, and behaviour. Some also support features like chat history, course-specific knowledge, or defining a “source of truth” so the AI responds using information relevant to your site.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Before implementing a chatbot, it’s worth thinking about a few practical questions:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">What kinds of questions should the chatbot answer?</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Should it support learners, instructors, or both?</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">How will you manage privacy, data usage, and AI governance?</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Moodle’s </span><a href="https://docs.moodle.org/en/AI_tools" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">AI Subsystem</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is designed to give organisations </span><b>choice and control over how AI is used</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">, including which AI providers you connect and where AI appears in your platform.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you’d like to explore what’s possible, you can learn more about Moodle’s approach to AI here: </span><a href="https://moodle.com/solutions/moodle-and-ai/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>Moodle and AI solutions</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">And if you’re thinking about bringing AI into your Moodle platform but aren’t sure where to begin, </span><a href="https://moodle.com/get-moodle/?utm_source=moodle_mentor&amp;utm_medium=blog&amp;utm_campaign=edit_mode"><span style="font-weight: 400;">our experts can advise you</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> on an approach that fits your technical setup, policies, and learning goals.</span></p>
</div>
</div>

    <div class=" flexi-block">        <h2 id="block-h2-when-your-moodle-site-talks-to-everything">When your Moodle site talks to everything</h2>
  <div class="quote">
    <div class="text">We are currently exploring ways to integrate Moodle with open-source ITSM and ITAM solutions (like GLPI) to create a seamless professional environment for our customers. We believe Moodle&#8217;s future lies in becoming a &#8216;Learning Hub&#8217; that talks to other business tools. We&#8217;d love to see more native API support or plugins that allow asset management and support ticketing to happen directly within the Moodle course interface to improve the user experience for corporate learners.
</div>
          <div class="name">Elavarasan M. S.</div>
      </div>
  <div class="text"><p><b>Moodle Mentor says:</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">You’re tapping into a really important idea here.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Many organisations today see their LMS not just as a place where courses live, but as a learning hub that connects to the rest of their digital ecosystem — from HR platforms to ticketing systems to asset management tools.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Moodle platforms already support this vision in a few important ways:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Open APIs </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">— Moodle LMS has a <a href="https://docs.moodle.org/dev/Web_service_API_functions" rel="noopener" target="_blank">robust web services API</a> that allows other systems to exchange data with the platform.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Plugins and integrations</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> —</span> <span style="font-weight: 400;"><a href="https://moodle.org/local/plugins/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Thousands of plugins</a> extend Moodle solutions to connect with HR systems, analytics tools, content providers, and many other services.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Flexible architecture </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">— Because Moodle LMS is open source, organisations and partners regularly build custom integrations that connect learning to other operational systems.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For corporate environments in particular, </span><a href="https://moodle.com/products/workplace/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>Moodle Workplace</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is designed to support structured programs, advanced reporting, and integrations across the organisation. Workplace is built on top of Moodle LMS and often becomes part of a broader ecosystem of business tools.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Ideas like yours are exactly the kind of thinking that shapes the future of the platform. If you’re exploring this direction, a couple of good places to connect with others working on similar ideas are the </span><a href="https://moodle.org/course/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>Moodle community forums</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and the </span><a href="https://moodledev.io/general/development/tracker" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><b>Moodle Tracker</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, where you can share suggestions or feature requests with the wider community.</span></p>
</div>
